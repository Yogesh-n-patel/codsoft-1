A Life of Purpose

Born on December 28, 1937, into the illustrious Tata family, Ratan Tata didn't just inherit a business empire—he transformed it into a global phenomenon while maintaining the family's legacy of ethical business and philanthropy.

After completing his education at Cornell and Harvard, he joined the family business in 1961, working his way up from the shop floor to become Chairman of Tata Sons in 1991. Under his leadership, the Tata Group's revenues grew from $5 billion to over $100 billion.

But beyond the numbers, what truly defines Ratan Tata is his unwavering commitment to India's development and his compassionate leadership style that puts people before profits.

Milestones in His Journey

1937

Born in Mumbai to Naval Tata and Soonoo Commissariat

1962

Joins Tata Steel, working on the shop floor in Jamshedpur

1991

Becomes Chairman of Tata Sons, begins transforming the conglomerate

2000

Acquires UK's Tetley Tea, marking Tata's first major international acquisition

2008

Leads acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover and shows remarkable leadership during Mumbai attacks

2012

Steps down as Chairman but continues guiding the group as Chairman Emeritus

2024

Passed away at the age of 86 in Mumbai. Honored with a state funeral, his legacy continues to inspire generations.

His Enduring Legacy

Ratan Tata's impact extends far beyond business. His leadership style—combining sharp business acumen with deep compassion—has redefined corporate India.

During the 2008 Mumbai attacks, he personally visited the Taj Hotel (a Tata property), stood with employees, and ensured all affected staff were cared for. This moment epitomizes his human-centered approach.

Through Tata Trusts, he championed initiatives in healthcare, education, and rural development. His support for startups and young entrepreneurs through investments and mentorship reflected his deep commitment to India's future.
